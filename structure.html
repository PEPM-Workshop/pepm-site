PEPM Organizational Structure

PEPM is run in accordance with the ACM SIGPLAN conference guidelines. The organizational structure of PEPM consists of a Steering Committee, and, for each work, a pair of program co-Chairs. Additional appointed roles support the co-Chairs in organizing the conference.

Steering Committee

The purpose of the PEPM Steering Committee is to provide continuity between one workshop and the next. The Steering Committee follows the ACM SIGPLAN Guidelines for Conference Steering Committees.

The main tasks of the Steering Committee are:

  • To act as PEPM's voice with the sponsoring organisation (ACM SIGPLAN).
  • To consult with the SIGPLAN Chair on matters of policy and planning.
  • To appoint co-Chairs for the conference each year in consultation with the SIGPLAN chair.
  • To offer advice to the co-Chairs.

The Steering Committee will formally meet at least once a year, preferably in person on the evening of the workshop, or online shortly after.

Membership

Membership of the Steering Committee is determined by the following rules:

  • The co-Chairs from future and recent conferences will be members of the Steering Committee. When a person accepts the position of chair for PEPM(n), they become a member of the Steering Committee until the PEPM(n+2) meeting.
  • Several members of the Steering Committee will be members at large, serving three-year terms. Each year, one member at large will be replaced with a new member, selected by the Steering Committee.

Program co-Chair

The program co-Chairs appoint the Program Committee. Its membership must be approved by the SIGPLAN Chair. The Steering Committee must be given an opportunity to offer suggestions, and comments on a proposed Program Committee, but the decision is taken by the co-Chairs, not the Steering Committee. The Program Committee should follow the ACM SIGPLAN policies regarding reviews and any special policies established by the co-Chairs.
